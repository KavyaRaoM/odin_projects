Paneer Butter Masala

Description

Paneer Butter Masala is a beloved Indian dish known for its rich and creamy texture and flavorful taste. It features paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cubes simmered in a luscious tomato-based gravy made with butter, cream, and a blend of aromatic spices. The sauce is typically prepared with onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a variety of spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander, which contribute to its deep, complex flavor. The dish is mildly spiced, making it a favorite among those who enjoy creamy and mildly spiced curries.

Often served with naan, roti, or rice, Paneer Butter Masala is a popular choice in Indian restaurants and homes alike. Its smooth and rich gravy, combined with the soft, succulent paneer, offers a delightful contrast in textures and flavors. This dish is perfect for special occasions or when you want to indulge in a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Paneer
  • Butter
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger_Garlic paste
  • heavy cream
  • turmeric powder
  • coriander powder

Steps

  1. If using fresh paneer, you can soak it in warm water for 15-20 minutes to keep it soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent and golden brown.
  3. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add Kashmiri red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook the spices for 1-2 minutes until aromatic.
  5. Pour in the tomato puree and mix thoroughly. Cook the mixture on medium heat until the oil starts to separate from the gravy, stirring occasionally.
  6. Lower the heat and add the remaining butter. Stir in sugar (if using) and adjust salt to taste. Simmer the gravy for 8-10 minutes until it thickens and the flavors meld together.
  7. Gently add the cubed paneer to the gravy and mix well, ensuring the paneer is coated evenly with the sauce. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to heat the paneer through.
  8. Pour in the heavy cream and stir gently to incorporate it into the gravy. Cook for 1-2 minutes more, being careful not to let the cream boil excessively.
  9. Garnish Paneer Butter Masala with chopped fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
